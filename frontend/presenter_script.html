💡 Part 3: Our Solution ⏱️ 2-3 min

"Our Voice Signal Integrity System has three main modules:"

Module 1: AI Voice Cloning Detection

"The first module uses Machine Learning to classify audio as REAL or FAKE.

Here's how it works: When you speak, your voice has unique acoustic characteristics - like a fingerprint. We extract these characteristics using something called MFCCs - Mel-Frequency Cepstral Coefficients.

In simple terms, MFCCs capture how sound 'shapes' match human hearing perception. AI-generated voices, no matter how good, have subtle differences in these acoustic patterns.

We trained a RandomForest machine learning model on hundreds of real and fake voice samples. The model learned to distinguish between them with high accuracy."

Module 2: Timbre Watermarking - Embedding

"The second module is our Timbre Watermarking system.

Timbre - pronounced 'tam-ber' - is what makes a guitar sound different from a piano playing the same note. It's the 'color' or 'quality' of sound, determined by its frequency content.

Our technique embeds ownership data - like a digital signature - directly into the audio's frequency spectrum. We do this using the Fast Fourier Transform, which converts audio from time domain to frequency domain.

The brilliant part? These changes are completely inaudible to humans. You cannot hear the difference between original and watermarked audio, but our system can detect and extract the hidden data."

Module 3: Watermark Extraction

"The third module can extract and verify the hidden watermark from any watermarked audio file.

Even if someone downloads your audio, edits it, or claims it as their own - you can use this module to prove your ownership by extracting the watermark you originally embedded."

🔧 Part 4: Technical Implementation ⏱️ 3-4 min

A. AI Detection - How MFCC Works

"Let me explain the technical process for AI detection:

Step 1: We load the audio file and resample it to 22,050 Hz - the standard sampling rate for audio analysis.

Step 2: We extract 20 MFCC coefficients. These coefficients represent different frequency bands of the audio on a scale that matches human hearing.

Step 3: We take the mean of each coefficient across time, giving us a 20-dimensional feature vector - essentially a 'fingerprint' of the audio.

Step 4: This vector is fed to our RandomForest classifier, which is an ensemble of 100 decision trees that vote on whether the audio is REAL or FAKE.

The model also provides a confidence score - showing how certain it is about its prediction."

B. Timbre Watermarking - The Push-Pull Strategy

"Now let me explain our watermarking technique:

Step 1: We convert the watermark text to binary bits.

Step 2: We apply Reed-Solomon error correction. This is the same technology used in CDs and QR codes - it adds redundancy so we can recover data even if some bits get corrupted.

Step 3: We split the audio into small chunks of 1024 samples each.

Step 4: For each chunk, we apply FFT - Fast Fourier Transform to convert from time to frequency domain.

Step 5: Here's the key innovation - our Push-Pull Strategy:
  • We select two frequency bin groups: Target bins around 1.5 kHz and Neighbor bins around 3 kHz
  • To encode a '1' bit: we boost Target energy and suppress Neighbor energy
  • To encode a '0' bit: we suppress Target energy and boost Neighbor energy

Step 6: We apply Inverse FFT to convert back to audio.

Step 7: We embed with 5x redundancy - each bit is written 5 times. During extraction, we use majority voting for reliability."

C. Why This Approach Works

"Three reasons why timbre watermarking is effective:

Imperceptibility: Humans cannot perceive small energy changes in specific frequency bins. The audio sounds exactly the same.

Robustness: Frequency domain modifications survive common audio processing like compression, format conversion, and minor edits.

Error Correction: Reed-Solomon encoding plus 5x redundancy means we can recover the watermark even if some data gets corrupted."

🏗️ Part 6: System Architecture ⏱️ 1-2 min

"Let me briefly explain our system architecture:

Frontend Layer: Built with HTML5, TailwindCSS, and JavaScript. Provides an intuitive user interface with three functional tabs.

API Layer: Python Flask server exposing three REST endpoints - /predict, /embed, and /verify.

Processing Layer:
